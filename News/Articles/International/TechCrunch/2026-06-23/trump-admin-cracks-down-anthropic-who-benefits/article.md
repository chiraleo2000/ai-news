## Summary

TechCrunch's Equity podcast analyzes the Trump administration's export control ban on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models, exploring political motivations, commercial implications, and the dangerous precedent for AI geopolitics.

---

## Body

The Trump administration issued an export control directive forcing Anthropic to take Fable 5 and Mythos 5 offline, citing national security concerns without public specifics. Key findings from TechCrunch's analysis:

**The trigger:** Amazon researchers found guardrail bypasses in Fable 5; Amazon CEO Andy Jassy reportedly raised concerns with the White House, which then issued the export control order on a Friday.

**Scope:** Anthropic pulled models entirely because it couldn't technically enforce "no foreign national access" at runtime.

**Expert backlash:** Cybersecurity professionals signed an open letter calling the ban counterproductive — it removes AI-powered defensive tools from U.S. network defenders. Similar bypasses reportedly exist in other AI models.

**Paradoxical commercial effect:** After the previous Anthropic-Trump feud, Claude downloads surged as users associated Claude with responsible/resistance AI. The brand may benefit even as operations suffer.

**Structural concern:** Anthropic has a uniquely poor relationship with the Trump administration compared to OpenAI and Google — suggesting political targeting as much as genuine security concern.
