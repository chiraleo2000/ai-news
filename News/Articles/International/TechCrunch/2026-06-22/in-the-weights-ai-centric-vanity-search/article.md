## Summary

A new tool called 'In the Weights' has launched as what TechCrunch describes as an AI-centric vanity search — letting people discover what large language models know about them, their companies, or their work based on what's embedded in model training data and weights. The tool represents a new genre of AI transparency product, addressing growing curiosity and concern about how public figures, researchers, developers, and companies are represented (or misrepresented) inside frontier AI models. As LLMs become gatekeepers of public knowledge, understanding your presence and portrayal in their weights becomes increasingly relevant for personal branding, reputation management, and research credibility.

## Tech Insights

**Impact:** AI vanity search tools reveal a transparency gap in LLM knowledge — organizations now need to audit their AI presence just as they monitor their web presence.

### Developer/Engineer Actions
- Use tools like In the Weights to check how your open-source projects and technical work are represented in major LLMs — inaccuracies affect discoverability and attribution.

### Business/Organization Actions
- Run an AI vanity search audit on your brand and key executives to identify misrepresentations in major LLMs before they affect customer or partner trust.
- Engage with AI model providers' feedback mechanisms if your organization is misrepresented in their training data knowledge.
