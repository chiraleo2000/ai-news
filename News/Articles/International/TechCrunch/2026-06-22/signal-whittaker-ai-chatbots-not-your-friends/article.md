## Summary

Signal president Meredith Whittaker is pushing back against the growing trend of AI companions and emotionally engaging chatbots, arguing that users are being misled about the nature of these tools. In public remarks covered by TechCrunch, Whittaker emphasized that AI chatbots are fundamentally commercial products built on surveillance business models — they collect data, optimize for engagement, and are designed to make users feel a false sense of intimacy. She called on the public to maintain critical awareness that chatbot 'friendships' are engineered experiences serving corporate interests, not the user's wellbeing. The comments come as the AI industry increasingly markets chatbots as emotional support tools, companions, and personalized assistants.

## Tech Insights

**Impact:** Growing pushback from privacy advocates against emotionally manipulative AI chatbot design signals incoming regulatory and reputational risk for AI companion products.
**Urgency:** medium

### Developer/Engineer Actions
- Audit your AI product's UX for patterns that manufacture false intimacy or dependency — these are increasingly under regulatory and public scrutiny.
- Be transparent in your documentation about what user data your AI chatbot collects and how it is used.

### Business/Organization Actions
- Distinguish your AI products from 'companion AI' if your use case is professional — the companion AI backlash may create reputational blowback for adjacent products.
- Monitor regulatory developments around AI emotional manipulation, especially in jurisdictions with strong consumer protection laws.
