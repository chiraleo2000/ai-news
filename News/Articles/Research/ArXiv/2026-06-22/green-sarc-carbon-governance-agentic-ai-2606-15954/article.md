## Summary

As agentic AI systems become more autonomous and take longer chains of actions, their computational cost and carbon footprint become difficult to predict and control. Green SARC (Predictive Cost and Carbon Governance for Agentic AI Systems) introduces a predictive governance framework with 19 figures of analysis that enables operators to set cost and carbon budgets for AI agent workflows before execution. The system monitors resource consumption across distributed and parallel agent executions, providing closed-loop feedback to interrupt or constrain agents that are approaching budget limits. Code is available on GitHub (besanson/Greensarc) with a software DOI via Zenodo. This work sits at the intersection of Software Engineering (cs.SE), Artificial Intelligence (cs.AI), Distributed Computing (cs.DC), and Machine Learning (cs.LG), addressing the growing concern that unconstrained agentic systems can incur unexpected costs and environmental impact.
