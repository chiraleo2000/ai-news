## Summary

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems for long documents face fundamental challenges when content spans multiple modalities (text, tables, figures) and requires multi-hop reasoning. MAGE-RAG (Multigranular Adaptive Graph Evidence for Agentic Multimodal RAG) proposes a graph-based evidence framework that operates at multiple granularity levels to capture both fine-grained and document-level relationships. The system builds adaptive evidence graphs that connect text, visual, and structured data, enabling agentic traversal for complex question answering tasks. By treating long-document QA as a graph retrieval and reasoning problem rather than a simple dense retrieval problem, MAGE-RAG addresses the cross-modal alignment challenge that degrades conventional RAG pipelines on complex real-world documents. The approach spans Information Retrieval (cs.IR), Computation and Language (cs.CL), Databases, and Multimedia domains.
